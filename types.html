Different Types of Disabilities

Disabilities come in many forms, and they affect people in different ways. Here are a few examples:

  • Physical Disabilities: These affect how a person moves or uses their body. Examples include people who use wheelchairs, crutches, or have conditions that affect their muscles.
  • Sensory Disabilities: These involve a person's senses. For example, people who are blind or have low vision, or people who are deaf or hard of hearing.
  • Learning Disabilities: These can affect how a person learns, understands, or processes information. Conditions like dyslexia (difficulty with reading) or ADHD (difficulty with attention and focus) are examples.
  • Developmental Disabilities: These are conditions that can affect a person's development, like autism spectrum disorder, which can impact how a person communicates and interacts with others.

Each type is unique, and understanding them helps us better support everyone in our community.
